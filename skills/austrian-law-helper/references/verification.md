# Verification Checklist

Legal output is verified by source, date, and jurisdiction discipline; a fluent answer with an unverified basis is a failure.

## Deterministic Floor
1. Every legal proposition cites a current official source (RIS for statutes and case law, the authority's own site for procedures) and the citation names the provision, not just the law.
2. Source currency: the consulted version's in-force date is checked against today's date and stated; amendments in transition are flagged.
3. Deadline arithmetic is recomputed independently: trigger event, delivery fiction rules, non-working-day shifts, and the resulting calendar date shown step by step.
4. Draft letters contain zero unresolved placeholders; names, dates, file numbers, and addresses are either filled from user-provided facts or explicitly marked for the user.
5. Jurisdiction check: the answer states whether federal law, Vienna state law, or municipal practice governs, and does not import German (DE) legal concepts.

## Evidence Checklist
- The confidence boundary is explicit: what follows from the cited source versus what depends on facts only the user or a professional can confirm.
- Escalation triggers are honored: representation requirements, court deadlines, and criminal exposure route to a recommendation for timely professional help.
- The user's own review before sending any letter is stated as mandatory, and any unresolved deadline or evidence risk is repeated at the end, not buried.
