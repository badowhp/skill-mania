# Austrian And Vienna Legal Sources

## Source Hierarchy

1. Use the current official text of the relevant statute, regulation, decision, or authority document.
2. Prefer official explanations for procedure, responsible authority, forms, and service channels.
3. Use reputable secondary guidance only to orient research; do not treat it as authority when a primary source is available.
4. State the source's status and retrieval date. A consolidated text is useful for reading, but the official promulgation rules can matter for binding effect and transitional provisions.

## Official Starting Points

- [Rechtsinformationssystem des Bundes (RIS)](https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/UI/Info.aspx): federal law, state law, case law, and official publication information.
- [Austrian government legal and administrative information](https://www.oesterreich.gv.at/themen/gesetze_und_recht.html): procedure-oriented information and responsible-service guidance.
- [Wiener Rechtsinformationen](https://www.wien.gv.at/recht/): Vienna state and municipal legal information, publication material, and links to current Vienna law.
- [Wiener Rechtsvorschriften](https://www.wien.gv.at/recht/landesrecht-wien/rechtsvorschriften/): current Vienna state and municipal rules and RIS entry points.
- [Austrian justice service](https://www.justiz.gv.at/): court procedure, forms, and current service information.

## Research Method

- Search the legal title, section, authority, and fact pattern in RIS. Open the result and verify the version or effective-date information against the relevant event date.
- Check whether the rule is federal, Vienna state, municipal, EU, or contractual. Do not apply Vienna law merely because the user lives in Vienna.
- Check transitional provisions, exceptions, procedural rules, and the decision or notice itself before describing a deadline.
- Cite the legal title, provision, current or historical version as applicable, official link, and retrieval date. Explain plainly what still depends on facts or professional interpretation.

## Correspondence Safeguards

- Preserve the original notice and proof of delivery before drafting a response. Do not alter documents or advise ignoring a notice.
- Put the recipient, reference number, date, subject, factual request, attachments, and preferred reply channel in a draft when they are known.
- Use neutral phrasing such as a request for clarification, file access, extension, confirmation of receipt, or review only when it matches the user's verified objective. Mark placeholders instead of guessing.
- Tell the user to review every factual statement, attachment, amount, and deadline before sending. Sending a draft remains the user's decision.

## Urgent Matters

- For police contact, criminal accusations, custody, violence, detention, removal or residence risk, court hearings, enforcement, eviction, or a short statutory or authority deadline, state that urgent Austrian legal help is needed and preserve documents immediately.
- If there is immediate danger, direct the user to emergency services. Do not provide tactical advice about evading authorities, destroying evidence, concealing assets, or avoiding legal obligations.
- For inability to afford court proceedings, check current official information about `Verfahrenshilfe`; eligibility and coverage are case-specific.
