# Generative Search Reference
Use this for AI Overviews, answer-engine optimization, GEO, answer inclusion, entity clarity, citation readiness, freshness, evidence, and brand visibility in AI answers.
## Standards
- Treat GEO as search visibility plus answer usefulness, not a separate bag of tricks. Start with SEO, crawlability, and helpful content.
- For Google AI Overviews and AI Mode, do not invent special technical requirements: current official guidance says ordinary Search eligibility and SEO fundamentals remain the foundation.
- Make pages entity-clear: name the company, product, category, audience, location, dates, authors, credentials, and source of claims where relevant.
- Add original evidence where possible: first-hand observations, benchmarks, pricing facts, case details, quotes, tables, FAQs, methodology, screenshots, or examples.
- Structure content for extraction without fragmenting it unnaturally: answer blocks, descriptive headings, comparison tables, definitions, pros/cons, and source-backed claims.
- Keep facts fresh and timestamped when freshness matters. Do not invent statistics or authority signals.
- Use `llms.txt` only when a target platform or team explicitly wants it. Do not present it as required for Google Search or as a substitute for crawlable HTML.
- Do not invent AI-specific schema or machine-readable files. Use structured data that matches visible content and a supported user-facing purpose.
- For brand visibility in AI answers, prefer authoritative owned pages plus real third-party proof over inauthentic mentions.
- Distinguish Search inclusion controls from model-training controls. Verify the crawler and platform semantics before recommending robots or preview directives.
## Checklist
- Does visible content answer a real query better than commodity summaries?
- Are claims attributable, current, and supported by visible evidence?
- Would an answer engine extract the correct entity, answer, source, and next action?
- Are citations, author/source signals, dates, and methodology visible where they matter?
- Is measurement tied to business outcomes and platform-native reporting rather than unsupported visibility claims?
